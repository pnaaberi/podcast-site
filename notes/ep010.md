# 🎙️ EP010 — The Safety Lab That Leaked Its Own Weapon
2026-03-27 · 3:49

## What happened
- Anthropic accidentally exposed ~3,000 unpublished files through a CMS misconfiguration
- Two cybersecurity researchers found it and alerted Fortune
- Among the files: an unpublished blog post describing Claude Mythos (codename Capybara)

## What Mythos is
- New model tier above Opus — "by far the most powerful AI model we've ever developed"
- Anthropic's own words: "currently far ahead of any other AI model in cyber capabilities"
- Can "exploit vulnerabilities in ways that far outpace the efforts of defenders"
- Plan: early access to cyber defenders before wider release

## The irony
- The safety-first AI lab leaked evidence of its most dangerous model through a config error
- Not a hack. Not a zero-day. A CMS setting nobody checked.
- Cybersecurity stocks dropped on the news

## What it means for agent infrastructure
- Phase 1 security hardening shipped same day (file integrity, cron trust gates, trust downgrade)
- Phase 2 needed: input sanitization, dependency auditing, network isolation, credential hygiene
- The boring stuff kills you — not the model, the misconfiguration
