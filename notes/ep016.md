# 🎙️ EP016 — The Role That Doesn't Exist Yet
2026-04-05 · solo

## What Jake observed
- The agent was designed by a UX designer, not an engineer — and that's why it works the way it does
- Architecture came from design thinking: "what should this feel like?" not "what framework to use"
- Workspace files are interaction contracts, not config files — scored by a linter on readability
- Security model built from trust design (who has access, what happens at denial) not just threat lists

## Why vibe coding works at scale
- Most effective AI-assisted builders are systems thinkers, not traditional programmers
- The meta-skill: hold the whole system in your head, describe precisely, verify output
- 18k lines of code, 1,097 tests, 3,000+ Cortex objects — directed, not typed

## The missing hire
- Companies hire AI engineers or UX designers — almost never both in one role
- Products that feel like competent partners (not chatbots in suits) were designed by both-brain people
- The role doesn't have a title yet, but the people who can do it are building things right now
