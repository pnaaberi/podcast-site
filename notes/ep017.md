## What Renky actually is
- Custom agent runtime on Claude Agent SDK — systemd service, Telegram interface, SQLite-backed knowledge graph (Cortex)
- 18K lines TypeScript, 1000+ tests, 2700 Cortex objects, built in 6 weeks by one UX designer + one AI agent
- Persistence is the difference: sessions survive reboots, cron jobs fire at 4 AM, sub-agents spawn in isolated git worktrees

## CLAUDE.md vs runtime
- CLAUDE.md = instructions pasted into context window, forgotten between sessions
- Renky = infrastructure that never stops — knowledge graph, cron scheduling, proactive messaging, session state across reboots
- "A CLAUDE.md is a script. Renky is infrastructure."

## The loom analogy
- Catalini (Forbes, 2026): "Babysitting the Slop" — the Measurability Gap
- 1842 Lowell textile mills added a third loom per weaver → output didn't increase 50%, it collapsed
- The weaver's real job wasn't making cloth — it was watching cloth get made (monitoring for broken threads, defects, micro-adjustments)
- Third loom exceeded human verification bandwidth → mills cut speed 15%, took a year of retraining
- By 1902: one weaver managed 18 looms (50x output vs a century earlier) — but only after 3x training investment per worker
- Bessen's finding: 62% of remaining productivity gains came from better-skilled humans monitoring, not better machines
- "The binding constraint was never the loom. It was the weaver's ability to verify the loom's output."
- Today the loom is an AI agent. The constraint hasn't changed. Renky's babysitter pattern, BUILD-CHECKLIST, and correction loops are the modern equivalent of training the weaver.
