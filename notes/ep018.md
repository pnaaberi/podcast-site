# 🎙️ EP018 — The Measurability Gap
2026-04-01 · 10:51

## The cost to generate is a rocket. The cost to verify is a bicycle.
- Catalini, Hui, and Wu define the Measurability Gap: AI generation costs fall exponentially, but verification costs stay flat
- "Every time you make generation cheaper without making verification cheaper, you just create more unverified output"
- SWE-bench went from under 2% to over 70% in about a year. Google says 25% of new code is AI-generated. But who's checking it?

## 1842 Lowell mills predicted this
- Weavers got a third loom — per-loom output dropped, quality suffered. The bottleneck wasn't weaving, it was watching.
- By the early 1900s: one weaver managed 16+ looms. Not from better looms — from 3x training investment per worker.
- 62% of productivity gains came from better-skilled human monitors, not better machines (Bessen, Learning by Doing)

## How to close the gap
- Standardized verification (BUILD-CHECKLIST.md) — not vibes, a checklist
- Monitoring specialization (babysitter pattern) — two-pass sub-agent review with automatic retry
- Institutional memory (Cortex) — every mistake becomes a searchable rule that prevents the class, not just the instance
- "You're not hiring the weaver. You're hiring the person who trained the weaver to watch sixteen looms."

## The actionable takeaway
- Invest more in verification infrastructure than generation capability — the model is good enough
- Treat every correction as a capital investment: fix + rule > fix alone
- "The most undervalued capability in tech right now: directing, evaluating, and systematizing verification"
