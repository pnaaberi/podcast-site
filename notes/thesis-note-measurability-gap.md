## Key Finding
62% of productivity gains came from better-skilled humans monitoring machines, not from better machines. (Bessen's reconstruction of 1842-1902 Lowell textile mill data.)

## Core Concept
The Measurability Gap: two cost curves moving in opposite directions.
- Cost to generate (AI output) → falling exponentially
- Cost to verify (human checking) → flat, bounded by expertise and time
- "One curve is a rocket. The other is a bicycle."
- The widening gap is where economic value dies.

## Relevance to Thesis
- Directly supports the argument that AI-assisted UX development requires structured verification, not just better models
- The vibe coding workflow (human strategy + AI implementation + human review) is a verification bandwidth system
- Renky's BUILD-CHECKLIST, babysitter pattern, and correction loops are formal verification infrastructure
- Connects to thesis finding: "designer caught human-facing UX issues AI never would" — this IS the verification bandwidth at work
- AEO (Agent Experience Optimization) is about making verification cheaper: structured data, semantic HTML, and agent protocols reduce the cost to verify that a site works for AI agents
